How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Culver City?

Kitchen remodel costs in the Culver City / West LA area typically fall into three ranges:

  • Cosmetic refresh (new paint, hardware, appliances, minor fixture updates): $15,000 - $30,000
  • Mid-range remodel (new cabinets, countertops, backsplash, lighting, one-wall layout change): $40,000 - $80,000
  • Full gut renovation (new layout, plumbing relocation, high-end materials, structural work): $80,000 - $150,000+

What drives cost up in LA: permit fees (Culver City requires a permit for electrical, plumbing, and structural changes), labor rates, and material choices. What drives it down: keeping the same layout (no plumbing moves), standard cabinet lines vs. custom, and phasing the work.

We give free on-site estimates with a line-item breakdown so you can see exactly where the money goes before you commit to anything.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

For a mid-range kitchen remodel in Culver City or West LA, expect:

  • Design and permit: 3-6 weeks
  • Demo and rough work (plumbing/electrical/framing): 1-2 weeks
  • Cabinets, counters, tile: 2-3 weeks
  • Fixtures, appliances, punch-list: 1 week

Total: 7-12 weeks from permit to done, depending on material lead times. We give you a hard schedule before we start and we track it weekly. "On time, on budget" is something we put in writing.

Do I need a permit for a kitchen remodel in Culver City?

Yes, for any work involving:

  • Electrical panel changes or adding circuits
  • Plumbing rerouting (sink, dishwasher drain, gas line)
  • Structural changes (removing a wall, adding a window)
  • Changes to the range hood exhaust

Cosmetic work (new cabinets over existing footprint, countertops, backsplash, painting) typically does not require a permit, but we verify case-by-case. We pull all permits for every project - this protects you when you sell the home.

How do I choose a kitchen remodel contractor in West LA?

Four things to check before you sign anything:

  1. CSLB license - verify it at cslb.ca.gov. New Cali: #1008892, General Building "B," Active since 2014.
  2. Reviews - look at Yelp, Google, and Houzz together, not just one platform. Volume and recency matter.
  3. Communication style - ask how they handle change orders, delays, and decisions. You'll be in contact weekly for 2-3 months.
  4. Occupied-home experience - kitchen remodels in occupied homes require real planning. We've done hundreds of them.
